Wingene focuses on OKAN education for Ukrainian children: "Fulfilling the right to education."

For the last three weeks, the Social Welfare Office of Wingene, in partnership with the Driespan School Community, has provided OKAN (Integration Classes for Non-Dutch Speaking Students) education in the municipality. They aim to ensure the right to education, despite the added pressure on the education system, as stated by Tom Braet, Chairman of the Special Committee for Social Services (CD&V).

€193,727 in subsidies for the renovation of schoolyards in the Driespan School Community.

The Driespan School Community has received €193,727 in subsidies for the renovation of schoolyards as part of a €20 million investment wave for 130 school building projects in Flanders. Education Minister Ben Weyts has allocated a total of €3 billion for school construction during this government term. This investment also benefits the communities of Wingene and Zwevezele. The schools in the Driespan School Community are receiving the subsidy through the Agency for Infrastructure in Education (AGION). This is seen as a valuable investment in the future of children and is positively welcomed in the municipality.

Three school communities join forces for the resumption of classes.

The school communities of Ferdinand Verbiest (Meulebeke, Pittem), PoelbergOmmeland (Tielt, Aarsele, Dentergem), and Driespan (Ruiselede, Wingene, and Zwevezele) will delay the start of classes by coordinating their approach. They will begin on Monday, May 18, instead of Friday, May 15. Classes for certain grades will be split into A and B groups, with different days for attendance. Meals are canceled, and extracurricular activities are suspended. Childcare arrangements are still a concern for school administrations, requiring significant efforts. The dedication of school teams is appreciated, and everyone hopes for a successful start.
